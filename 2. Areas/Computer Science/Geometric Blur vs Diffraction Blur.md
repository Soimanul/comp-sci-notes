## Definition
Geometric blur and diffraction blur are two distinct causes of image blurring in pinhole and lens imaging systems.

## Core Ideas
- **Geometric blur** arises from finite aperture size allowing multiple rays from a single point to reach the image plane.\

- **Diffraction blur** arises from the wave nature of light; as aperture size shrinks, wave spreading becomes significant.

- Total blur is a combination of both phenomena; minimizing total blur requires balancing them.

## Mathematical Contrast
- Geometric blur diameter increases roughly with aperture size.

- Diffraction blur diameter increases as aperture size decreases, approximately as:
$$
\text{diffraction blur} \propto \frac{\lambda f}{d}
$$
where:
- $\lambda$ is wavelength,
- $f$ is focal length,
- $d$ is aperture diameter.

The total blur is the sum (or convolution) of the individual blur effects in many models.
