## Definition
Formal language theory (associated with Chomsky) treats language as a mathematical object defined by a grammar that can generate an infinite set of sentences from a finite set of rules.

## Core claims
- A language can be described independently of how frequently its sentences occur in real usage.
- The primary goal is to characterize what strings are well-formed and what structural descriptions they have.

## Impact on early NLP
This view shaped early computational NLP around parsing and grammaticality: designing grammars, deriving parse trees, and modeling hierarchical structure and recursion.
