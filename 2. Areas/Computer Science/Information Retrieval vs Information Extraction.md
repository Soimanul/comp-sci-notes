## Definition
While both fields deal with obtaining information from text, they differ in their goals and output.

### Information Retrieval (IR)
Focuses on **selecting relevant documents** from a collection. The output is an ordered list of documents. The internal content of the documents is not analyzed beyond what is needed for ranking.

### Information Extraction (IE)
Focuses on **identifying and structuring specific pieces of information** within documents, such as entities, relations, or events. The goal is to transform unstructured text into structured representations (e.g., a database).

## Significance
IR helps find *where* the information might be, while IE extracts the 
*what* within those sources.
