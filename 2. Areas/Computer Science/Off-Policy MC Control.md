## Definition

Off-Policy MC Control separates the **behavior policy** $b$ (the exploratory policy used to generate episodes) from the **target policy** $\pi$ (the deterministic greedy policy being learned and improved). The agent follows $b$ to collect data while learning and improving $\pi$.

> [!info] Key Intuition
> On-policy methods compromise: they learn the best *exploring* policy. Off-policy methods avoid this compromise — the target policy can be deterministic and optimal, while the behavior policy remains stochastic and exploratory.

## Two-Policy Structure

| | On-Policy | Off-Policy |
|---|---|---|
| Data generation policy | Same as target ($\pi$) | Behavior policy $b \neq \pi$ |
| Target policy | $\varepsilon$-soft (must explore) | Can be deterministic (greedy) |
| Exploration mechanism | $\varepsilon$-soft selection | Behavior policy handles it |
| Correction needed | None | Importance Sampling |

**Coverage requirement**: for learning about $\pi$ from $b$, every action taken under $\pi$ must also be taken under $b$: $\pi(a|s) > 0 \Rightarrow b(a|s) > 0$.

## Algorithm — Off-Policy MC Control

```
Initialize for all s,a:
  Q(s,a) ∈ ℝ arbitrarily
  C(s,a) ← 0
  π(s) ← argmax_a Q(s,a)   (ties broken consistently)

Loop forever (each episode):
  b ← any soft policy with coverage of π
  Generate episode following b: S₀,A₀,R₁,...,S_{T-1},A_{T-1},R_T
  G ← 0
  W ← 1
  Loop for t = T-1, T-2, ..., 0:
    G ← γG + R_{t+1}
    C(S_t, A_t) ← C(S_t, A_t) + W
    Q(S_t, A_t) ← Q(S_t, A_t) + W/C(S_t,A_t) * [G - Q(S_t,A_t)]
    π(S_t) ← argmax_a Q(S_t, a)
    If A_t ≠ π(S_t): break inner loop (proceed to next episode)
    W ← W * 1/b(A_t|S_t)
```

The inner loop exits early (via `break`) when the taken action $A_t$ differs from the greedy action $\pi(S_t)$, because from that point forward the trajectory is irrelevant for the deterministic target policy.

## Advantages of Off-Policy

- The target policy can be deterministic (optimal), not limited to $\varepsilon$-soft policies.
- Can learn from data generated by any policy — including a human expert or a legacy controller.
- More general: on-policy is the special case where $b = \pi$.
- Enables learning multi-step predictive models of environment dynamics.

## Disadvantages

- Off-policy methods are of greater variance and converge more slowly than on-policy methods.
- Require additional bookkeeping (importance sampling weights $W$, cumulative weight $C$).

> [!warning] Common Misconception
> The behavior policy $b$ and target policy $\pi$ being different does not mean the agent is doing something unreliable. The Importance Sampling correction mathematically transforms returns from the $b$-distribution to the $\pi$-distribution, making the estimates valid for $\pi$ despite being generated by $b$.

## Significance

Off-policy MC control is the foundation for Q-learning and DQN, which are off-policy TD methods. The off-policy paradigm is considered key to learning multi-step world models and to reusing historical experience (experience replay).
