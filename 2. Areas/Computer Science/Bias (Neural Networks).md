## Definition
An extra parameter ($b$) added to the weighted sum of inputs in a neuron. It allows the activation function to be shifted to the left or right.

## Analogy
In the linear equation $y = mx + b$, the bias is the **y-intercept**.

- Without bias, the separating line (decision boundary) must always pass through the origin $(0,0)$.
    
- Bias allows the neuron to fit data that is not centered at the origin.
