# Definition

The ELIZA Effect is the tendency for humans to unconsciously **anthropomorphize** computer systems, attributing human-like intelligence, emotions, and understanding to software that is actually operating on simple logic.

# Origin

The term was coined after users (including Weizenbaum's own staff) became emotionally attached to the [[ELIZA]] program. They would spill their secrets to it and ask Weizenbaum to leave the room so they could have "private" conversations with the machine.

# Implications for AI

- **Illusion of Competence:** Users often assume an AI understands the _meaning_ of their words simply because it produces grammatically correct responses.
    
- **Critical Evaluation:** It serves as a warning against overestimating the capabilities of NLP systems. Just because a model (like GPT-4) produces coherent text does not inherently mean it possesses sentience or deep understanding.
