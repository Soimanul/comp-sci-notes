## Definition
The circle of confusion is the blur disc formed by an out-of-focus point.

## Core Ideas
- Used as a threshold for acceptable sharpness

## Mathematical Criterion
A point is considered “in focus” if:

$$
b < c
$$

Where:
- $b$: blur diameter
- $c$: maximum acceptable circle of confusion (e.g., one pixel)
