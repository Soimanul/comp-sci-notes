## Definition

An optical defect where the lens fails to focus all colors to the same convergence point, causing colored fringes (halos) along edges in high-contrast areas.

## Core Cause

**Dispersion:** The refractive index of the glass depends on the wavelength of light ($\lambda$).

- Blue light (shorter $\lambda$) refracts more than red light (longer $\lambda$).
    
- As a result, different colors have slightly different focal lengths.
    

## Impact

- Reduces image sharpness.
    
- Creates color artifacts (purple/green fringing).
