## Definition
Language exhibits variation: words appear in different inflected or derived forms while expressing related meanings. Stemming and lemmatization reduce this variation by mapping related forms to a common representation. This increases statistical robustness at the cost of linguistic precision.

## Approaches
- [[Stemming]] 
- [[Lemmatization]] 

## Tradeoffs
Stemming prioritizes statistical simplicity, while lemmatization preserves more linguistic structure.
