## Definition
The semantic gap is the mismatch between having a correct syntactic structure (a valid parse) and having a representation of meaning. Syntax can describe how a sentence is formed, but it does not necessarily encode the underlying relations needed to determine “who did what to whom”.

## What the gap looks like in practice
- Different surface forms can express the same meaning.
- Similar grammatical structures can yield different interpretations.
- A parse tree can be correct while still leaving key semantic relations implicit or ambiguous.

## Why it matters
The semantic gap motivates meaning representations that make semantic roles and relations explicit, rather than treating syntactic well-formedness as sufficient for understanding.
