## Definition
Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) is a graph-based meaning representation where sentences are mapped to a structured graph of concepts and relations, aiming to represent underlying meaning independently of surface syntax.

## Key idea
AMR separates meaning from phrasing: if two sentences convey the same proposition, they should map to the same (or highly similar) AMR graph even if their syntactic structures differ.

## What AMR makes explicit
- Core events or predicates
- Participants and roles (agent, patient, etc.)
- Relations between concepts

## Cost and implication
AMR increases semantic transparency, but requires non-trivial parsing and often relies on annotated resources or complex mapping procedures.
