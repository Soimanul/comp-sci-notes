## Definition
A morpheme is the smallest meaningful unit in a language. Unlike a word, which can stand alone, a morpheme may or may not stand alone, but it always carries semantic meaning.

## Types of Morphemes

1. **Free Morphemes:** Can stand alone as independent words.
    - _Examples:_ "dog", "run", "table".
        
2. **Bound Morphemes:** Must be attached to other morphemes; they cannot stand alone. These are typically prefixes and suffixes.
    - _Examples:_ "un-" (negation), "-s" (plural), "-ed" (past tense).

## Role in NLP
Traditional linguistics focuses on morphemes to understand word formation. In NLP, techniques like [[Stemming]] attempt to approximate morphological analysis by stripping suffixes.
