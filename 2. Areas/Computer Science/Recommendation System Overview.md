## Definition

A **recommendation system** (reco) is an AI system that predicts the **affinity** between a user and an item and presents the most relevant items to each user, personalising experience at scale.

Every recommendation system has three fundamental components:
- **User** — a customer who interacts with the company and receives recommendations.
- **Item** — a product, content piece, or entity that can be recommended.
- **Interaction (Feedback)** — a record of a user-item engagement (purchase, click, rating, view time, etc.).

> [!info] Key Intuition
> A recommendation system behaves like a world-class salesperson: it finds the unmet, unarticulated needs of customers and offers the right product at the right time — but does this at internet scale for millions of users simultaneously.

## Core Goal

> "Our job is to meet the unmet unarticulated needs of our customers." — Satya Nadella

The system grows a business by creating a **personal relationship with every customer at scale**.

> [!example]- Worked Example
> On Netflix, instead of showing all 15,000 titles equally, the reco surfaces the 10–20 titles most likely to be watched by *this specific user* based on their history and similarity to other users. Result: 75% of viewing comes from recommendations, not manual browsing.

## Significance

Recommendation systems are consistently rated the AI solution with the **highest ROI** because they directly increase revenue, engagement, and customer satisfaction without requiring users to know what to search for.
