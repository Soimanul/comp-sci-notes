## Definition
The pinhole camera is an idealized camera model where light passes through a single small aperture and forms an inverted image.

## Core Ideas
- Each scene point maps to a single image point
- Produces sharp images without lenses
- Performs perspective projection

## Mathematical Relation
Image coordinates follow from perspective projection:

$$
x = f \cdot \frac{X}{Z}
$$

$$
y = f \cdot \frac{Y}{Z}
$$

## Limitations
- Extremely low light capture
- Requires long exposure times
