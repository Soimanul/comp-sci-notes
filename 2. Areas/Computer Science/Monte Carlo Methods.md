## Overview

Monte Carlo (MC) methods are model-free algorithms that learn value functions and optimal policies directly from sampled episodes of experience. Unlike Dynamic Programming, they require no knowledge of the environment's transition dynamics — only the ability to generate complete episodes. The family covers prediction (estimating $v_\pi$ or $q_\pi$ for a fixed policy) and control (finding $\pi_*$), and handles the exploration problem through either Exploring Starts or $\varepsilon$-soft policies, with off-policy variants using Importance Sampling to learn from data generated by a different policy.

> [!abstract]- TL;DR
> Monte Carlo methods estimate value functions by averaging actual sampled returns from complete episodes, requiring no model of the environment. Control is achieved via Generalized Policy Iteration on action-value functions, with exploration maintained through Exploring Starts, $\varepsilon$-soft policies, or off-policy Importance Sampling.
